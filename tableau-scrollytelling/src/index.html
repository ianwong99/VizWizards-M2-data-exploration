üìä Salary Breakdown

Finally, we turn to the economics of professional basketball by examining how player compensation relates to shooting performance. In the top visualization, player salaries for each season are grouped into bins based on 3-point percentage (3P%), allowing you to compare how shooting efficiency aligns with pay within any given year. The bottom visualization shows how average salaries have evolved over time across the league.

As expected, overall player salaries rise steadily across the years, but an interesting pattern emerges after roughly 2015: higher-earning players increasingly cluster in the upper 3P% bins. In other words, the modern NBA places a growing premium on perimeter shooting, and many of the league's top earners consistently shoot above 30% from three.

However, this trend does not apply uniformly across all positions. For example, traditional centers (C) typically attempt fewer three-point shots, so their salary is less correlated with 3P%.
